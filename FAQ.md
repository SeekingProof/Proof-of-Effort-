# Proof of Effort — Frequently Asked Questions

**Positive proof your writing is human.** Proof of Effort (PoE) doesn't guess whether a machine wrote your words. It records the real human effort behind them as you write, sealed into evidence you can prove.

---

## A · What it is & how it's different

### What is Proof of Effort (PoE)?

PoE proves a document was genuinely written by a person. As you write, it records the effort inside your document, where it travels with the file. It watches how you write, not what you write, and never stores your text. Made by Khepri Humetric Solutions; available at SeekingProof.com.

### What is PoET?

PoET (Proof of Effort for Text) is the first PoE product — an add-on for LibreOffice Writer on Windows and macOS and soon MS Word. It records your writing effort, stores it in your document, and lets you score or seal it for sharing. PoE and PoET are used interchangeably. A music counterpart, PoEM, is in development.

### Is PoE an Ai detector?

No. PoE is a different kind of tool. Detectors guess whether a machine wrote your finished text; PoE never guesses or judges content — it records what actually happened as you wrote and lets that speak for itself.

### What makes PoE different?

Ai detectors work backward from finished words and produce an unexplainable probability that often flags honest writing. PoE works forward from the act of composing, recording real effort as it happens. Instead of a guess about your text, you get positive, attested proof of your effort.

### How does PoE work, in plain terms?

As you write, PoE reads the rhythm, edits, and flow of composition — the cognitive signals of genuine human writing — without your words and without the precise keystroke timing that could identify you. When you score, it produces a Total Effort Score out of 100, plus a typed-vs-pasted reading and a section-by-section transcription check.

### What is a .poe artifact, and when would I use one?

Usually you won't need one — your PoE data already lives in your document, so you just share the document. An artifact is optional: it pulls your effort record into a separate sealed .poe file, linked to your document, so you can prove your effort without sending an editable embedded record.

## B · What PoE doesn't do

### Does PoE identify who wrote the document?

No. It attests that human effort happened, not whose. The record holds no user, device, or network identifiers.

### Does PoE read the content of what I wrote?

No. It observes interaction types and timing — never the actual text.

### Is PoE biometric?

No — it's explicitly non-biometric. Traditional keystroke dynamics can identify you through precise timing; PoE deliberately omits that precision. Picture a sharp photo of your face (biometric) versus the rough shadow you cast (non-biometric): one is distinctly you, the other just distinctly human.

### Does PoE detect Ai-generated text or judge authorship?

No. It doesn't scan content for Ai signatures and makes no authorship determination. It measures effort, not output.

### Is PoE a bot detector?

No. Bot detection blocks or filters access in real time. PoE just produces a portable record of a writing session — it decides nothing.

### Does PoE decide whether someone cheated?

No. PoE is a measurement, not a verdict — closer to a forensic instrument. What an institution infers from it is their responsibility, not PoE's.

## C · Privacy, security & verification

### Is my privacy protected?

Yes. PoE never keeps your words — scoring uses only a mathematical representation of writing activity. Full details in the privacy policy at SeekingProof.com.

### Could someone reconstruct what I typed?

No. The record holds no keystrokes, words, or meaning. Reconstruction is impossible by design.

### Is PoE watching everything on my computer?

No. It only sees your writing activity inside the document you're working on, while you're working on it.

### Can a proof be faked or tampered with?

Tampering is detectable. Every record carries a cryptographic seal, so any change made after the fact breaks verification rather than passing silently. Changing the words breaks the match; changing fonts or formatting doesn't.

### How does someone verify my proof — and do they need to trust Khepri?

Verification is automatic and needs no server or special access: any conforming verifier confirms the record is intact and the document matches the session data. It's like a notarized file — a moment locked in time that anyone can check. (Scoring runs on Khepri's servers, so it can't be gamed.)

### Who can verify, and do they pay?

Anyone, free — verifying, scoring, and importing need no license. They can check a document carrying its embedded PoE data or a standalone .poe file.

## D · Getting started & requirements

### What do I need to use PoET?

LibreOffice Writer (free) on Windows or macOS, a PoET license, and an internet connection to score. Recording works offline; only scoring and importing need to be online. A paid license unlocks creation — verifying and scoring are free.

### Which platforms does PoET support?

LibreOffice Writer on Windows and macOS today. A Microsoft Word add-in arrives September/October 2026. Google Docs isn't supported. Anyone can install LibreOffice for free to verify your work.

### How do I install and activate it?

Open Tools ▸ Extension Manager, add the .oxt file, and restart LibreOffice. On Windows, accept the one-time keyboard-checker prompt. Enter the license code from your purchase email, and you're set.

### Can I use one license on multiple computers?

Each license activates one installation; you can add licenses for more computers (see Pricing). Replaced or reset a machine? Contact support — one courtesy re-activation per license is available.

### Can I convert my effort data to MS Word (.docx)?

Not yet — DOCX conversion is planned around the Word add-in release. For now your PoE data stays with the LibreOffice (.odt) file, which anyone can open and verify for free.

### Does it work with Grammarly, autocomplete, or voice-to-text?

PoET records real typing, so text inserted another way can't be verified. Autocorrect is fine; heavy autocomplete or Grammarly's phrase suggestions usually read as transcribed or unverified; voice-to-text isn't supported yet (keep an audio backup). Use what you like — just disclose it.

## E · Using it & scoring

### Where is my PoE data kept?

Inside your document, from the moment you open the file — it travels with it. Saving seals that session's record; closing without saving discards unsaved work and its record. To share proof, just share the document.

### What do the scores mean?

The Total Effort Score (out of 100) combines your editing behavior and cognitive engagement. Cognitive Engagement reflects genuine, thought-driven composition (shown as a Cognitive Waveform) — it's not an excellence meter; a higher score just means you write closer to average. Very low scores imply unusually precise or chaotic patterns. Typed vs. Pasted shows how much was typed directly; the Transcription Signal flags each section as Authentic, Transcribed, or Insufficient Data. Full detail is in the user guide.

### Can't someone just retype text to fool it?

No — that's transcription, and it leaves different cognitive signals than composing your own thoughts. PoET checks section by section and flags transcribed passages with a confidence level.

### What happens if I paste text?

Pasting is allowed and recorded. Pasted passages show in your Typed vs. Pasted result and lower the typed share. Moving your own already-typed text doesn't count against you — only outside content and pre-existing template text read as unverified, so disclose those.

### Can I use a keystroke injector to insert text?

You can. PoET won't stop you, but no effort data will be stored into your document, because no human effort occurred.

### Can I undo a mistake?

Before saving, yes — close without saving and the work and its record go together. After saving, the record is sealed; start fresh in a new document. Deleting text doesn't remove the underlying record.

### Will editing my document break the .poe file proof?

Formatting changes won't; changing the words will, because the proof is tied to the text. Just save again and generate a fresh proof.

### Do I need to be online?

Only to score or import. Recording works offline.

## F · Education & institutions

### Can schools or institutions use PoE?

Yes — it's built with education in mind. Because it never stores student text or identifies individuals, it supports academic-integrity goals without collecting sensitive personal data.

### How does PoE protect a writer accused of using Ai?

Ai detectors make educated guesses about the style of your content. PoE provides documentation of human cognitive effort during composition, which a teacher can verify without having to read through logs, watch time-lapse videos of you writing, or depend on an Ai's guess. PoE uses no Ai; it provides a verifiable record of the effort behind the writing.
